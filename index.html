Tar Sands Oil Spills

  • Why are tar sands oil spills worse? They are more difficult and costly to clean up, and they bring forth more serious health risks than conventional oil spills.*
  • Statistically, tar sands pipelines spill 3.6 times more often than the US average.*
  • The spill that cost the most per barrel to clean up in US history was the Kalamazoo River spill (from a tar sands oil pipeline). After two years and hundreds of millions of dollars, some areas around the spill still contain oil from the spill.*
  • TransCanada predicted that their Keystone 1 pipeline would spill once every seven years. In reality, the Keystone 1 spilled 12 times in just its first year of operation. Throughout it's lifetime it spilled an additional 30 times.*
  • The Keystone XL will cross 1,904 waterways. This includes the Ogallala Aquifer (the largest source of freshwater in US).*
  • Keystone XL spills are predicted to have an enormous and devastating affect on employment (see above source)
